Venezuelan Motherhood

three time 5 star award winner

Venezuelan families

Traditions

Venezuelan society is mostly matriarchal with exceptions in some regions of the country. This places mothers at the top of the hierarchy in the family nucleus, adding the fact that always being in contact with our extended families is the norm, grandmothers by default are the heads of very large families. When a woman's family gets married, her husband usually joins her family. Throughout the country there are still very strong traditions that surround motherhood from the beginning of pregnancy, until months after delivery. In the Venezuelan Andes traditions are strongly respected. Probably many of the younger people don't believe in them, but no one has the courage to contradict the older women in the family. Among the most curious traditions before childbirth are:

      Pregnant women are not allowed to enter the cemetery. It is applied up to several weeks after delivery.
      People take the new mothers to their homes so that, with the use of a chancla (Latina Mom's Instrument Of Torture, this will be the topic of the next entry ), they hit the fruit trees that have not wanted to give harvests and demand fruits for the next season.
      Pregnant women are not allowed to go out at night if el Sereno (wind) is too strong.

The traditions associated with postpartum are even stronger. The most practiced is la Cuarantena (quarantine) . After delivery, women are isolated with their babies for 40 days. For this to take place, pregnant women close to the time of giving birth move with their mothers, even if they live in other regions of the country. During this time, chicken soup is consumed daily, always using chicken feet, also tomato d palo juice to increase mother's milk production and mangoes are prohibited.

Normally at this time the closest neighbors help the family by giving the family large amounts of rice with chicken and fresh fruits with vitamin C like the Guayaba so that the women who take care of the new mother and her newborn get adequate food.
